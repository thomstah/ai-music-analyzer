## 3. DMCA Notice & Takedown Policy (template)

> **[Your Company Name] — DMCA Policy**
>
> We respect intellectual property rights and comply with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA). This page describes how to submit takedown notices and counter-notices.
>
> ### Submitting a Takedown Notice
>
> If you believe content on Lyriq infringes your copyright, please send a written notice containing ALL of the following (17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3)):
>
> 1. A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized agent
> 2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed
> 3. Identification of the allegedly infringing material on Lyriq, with sufficient detail (URL) to locate it
> 4. Your contact information (name, address, phone, email)
> 5. A statement that you have a good-faith belief that the use is not authorized
> 6. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information is accurate and you are authorized to act on the copyright owner's behalf
>
> Send notices to:
>
> > **[Designated Agent Name]**
> > [Your Company Name]
> > [Mailing address]
> > Email: dmca@yourdomain.com
>
> Our Designated Agent is registered with the U.S. Copyright Office. View registration at [https://www.copyright.gov/dmca-directory/].
>
> ### What Happens Next
>
> Upon receipt of a valid notice, we will:
> 1. Remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material promptly
> 2. Notify the user who posted/analyzed the material (if applicable)
> 3. Record the notice in our repeat-infringer log
>
> ### Counter-Notice
>
> If you believe content was removed in error, you may submit a counter-notice containing the elements required by 17 U.S.C. § 512(g)(3). We will forward the counter-notice to the original complainant and restore the material in 10–14 business days unless the complainant files a court action.
>
> ### Repeat Infringers
>
> We terminate the accounts of users who are determined to be repeat infringers.
>
> ### False Claims
>
> Submitting a false takedown notice is subject to penalties under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f). Do not abuse this process.

## Implementation checklist (before going public)

- [ ] Customize bracketed placeholders in each template
- [ ] Have an attorney review final versions (~$300–500 typical review fee)
- [ ] Register DMCA Designated Agent with U.S. Copyright Office ($6 fee, 15-minute process — https://dmca.copyright.gov/osp/)
- [ ] Publish Terms / Privacy / DMCA at `/terms`, `/privacy`, `/dmca`
- [ ] Add footer links to all three from every page
- [ ] Add checkbox "I agree to Terms and Privacy Policy" on signup
- [ ] Set up `dmca@yourdomain.com`, `privacy@yourdomain.com`, `legal@yourdomain.com` inboxes (can all forward to one address)
- [ ] Add visible AI-generated content disclaimer near each Claude interpretation
- [ ] Add cookie consent banner if serving EU users (free libraries available)
- [ ] Test the account deletion flow end-to-end (GDPR requirement)
